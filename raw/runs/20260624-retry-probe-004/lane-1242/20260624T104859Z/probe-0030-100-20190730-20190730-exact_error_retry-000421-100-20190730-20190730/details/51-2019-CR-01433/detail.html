2. 24/7 Sobriety Program - Ward, The Defendant will participate in (and pay all fees associated with) the 24/7 Sobriety Program, in accordance with the Sobriety Program guidelines the defendant is to obtain from the Ward County Sheriff's Office/Ward County Jail. The Defendant is specifically put on notice that non-compliance with any of the conditions of the 24/7 Sobriety Program will result in the defendant being taken into custody immediately, to await an appearance before a Judge to determine whether the defendant's probation should be revoked or modified in some manner, or remain in its present form. The defendant will be on this program for the first 360 days of his supervised probation. 01/23/2020 - 01/17/2021, Active 01/23/2020
3. Supervised Probation, 18 months (Extended to 11/3/2022) 01/23/2020, Closed 12/21/2021
4. Chemical Dependency Evaluation - Ward, The Defendant must report to a licensed addiction evaluator to undergo a chemical dependency evaluation and file proof with the Court by end date. The Defendant is required to follow all recommendations made by the evaluator; provided, however, that any recommended treatment shall not, without the Defendant's consent continue for longer than the probationary period. If the Defendant disagrees with the treatment recommendations, he/she may, within 10 days after receiving the recommendations, request a hearing before the court to assess the appropriateness of alternative treatment programs. The Defendant may be represented by counsel at such hearing. NOTE: The Defendant must execute a RELEASE OF INFORMATION FORM which will permit the evaluator to provide the court with the following information: (a) confirmation the Defendant has completed the court ordered evaluation; (b) a REPORT OF EVALUATION setting forth the results of each evaluation and the recommendations of the evaluator; (c) confirmation the Defendant has completed any recommended treatment. IT IS THE DEFENDANT'S RESPONSIBILITY TO MAKE CERTAIN THAT THE EVALUATOR(S) PROVIDE(S) THIS INFORMATION TO THE COURT IN A TIMELY MANNER. If the defendant is unable to obtain this evaluation while incarcerated, this will be due 45 days after release of incarceration. 01/23/2020 - 03/08/2020, , Satisfied 12/14/2021
5. Victim Impact Panel - Ward, Ward County Administration Building on February 5, 2020 @ 7:00 pm. The Defendant is to be transported to this Panel by the Ward County Jail. 01/23/2020, Satisfied 08/10/2020
6. Pay From Bond Posted - Ward, Any cash bond posted will be forfeited to fines, fees and restitution, in this file, and any other Ward County file which fines, fees and restitution is owed. Any remaining cash bond will be refunded to the bond remitter. $100 of the cash bond will be applied to fines and fees with the remainder being paid to Rehab Services for attendance of the Victim Impact Panel. 01/23/2020, Active 01/23/2020
7. Turn In License Plates (DUI) - Ward, The Defendant, having been convicted of a second or subsequent offense within seven years, pursuant to N.D.C.C. 39-08-01(4) is ordered to surrender immediately to the Ward County Sheriff the motor vehicle number plates of all the motor vehicles owned and operated by the defendant.
